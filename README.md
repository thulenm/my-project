**Project 1**

This project summarizes key insights from the Paris Summer Olympics 2024. This includes the medal table results by countries, disciplines and events. Further analysis into medallist information is also carried out. The main goal of the project is to provide highlights and interesting results from the world’s most prestigious sport event. 
The dataset for this project is taken from Kaggle. The data is already cleaned and pre-processed. I utilized Python for exploratory data analysis (EDA), employing libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn to visualize and interpret key trends and patterns in the dataset. 
Some key findings from the analysis: 
-	USA and China topped the medal table by having the same number of gold medals (40). However, USA secured the first place as it has more silver and bronze medals, making it the leading nation in total medal count.
-	14 out of 40 gold medals of USA was from Athletics, making it a stronghold for the nation. China got 8 gold medals from diving. 
-	The exploration of the highest and lowest ages of gold medallists from different disciplines shows that ages differ by sport: Athletes in sports that demands speed and power (Gymnastics, Sport Climbing) often reach the peak of their careers in their mid-20s, while athletes in tactical, precision sports with low physical loads such as Equestrian, Golf, Sailing even peaks around their 40s-50s. 

Detailed analysis is included in the Jupiter Notebook. 


**Project 2**

This project examines how daily digital habits — including social media usage, screen time, notification exposure and work-related characteristics such as work hours, coffee consumption, stress level relate to individual productivity.  
The dataset for this project is taken from Kaggle. The data is cleaned and pre-processed (handling missing values or outliers, imputation, converting variables). I utilized Python for exploratory data analysis (EDA), employing libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn to visualize and interpret key trends and patterns in the dataset. Also, I conducted a multiple linear regression to identify factors that explain variations in productivity score. 
Some key findings from the analysis: 
-	People do not actively find ways to manage their social media usage and maintain a healthy relationship with the phone, such as managing screen time and reducing distractions from work and study
-	People with different job types vary in working habits and social media using habits. For example, IT employees have highest average days feeling burnout per month, highest coffee consumption per day, but having highest average job satisfaction score. One possible reason for this is because they have the lowest average work hours per day. Moreover, they maintain a healthy social media using habit, by having the highest weekly offline hours (excluding sleep time) and spending the least time on screens before bed. 
-	Increasing time spent on social media reduces productivity score
-	Individuals who are satisfied with their job, are also highly likely to find themselves productive 

Detailed analysis is included in the Jupiter Notebook.

This project summarizes key insights from the Paris Summer Olympics 2024. This includes the medal table results by countries, disciplines and events. Further analysis into medallist information is also carried out. The main goal of the project is to provide highlights and interesting results from the world’s most prestigious sport event. 

The dataset for this project is taken from Kaggle. The data is already cleaned and pre-processed. I utilized Python for exploratory data analysis (EDA), employing libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn to visualize and interpret key trends and patterns in the dataset. 

Some key findings from the analysis: 
-	USA and China topped the medal table by having the same number of gold medals (40). However, USA secured the first place as it has more silver and bronze medals, making it the leading nation in total medal count.
-	14 out of 40 gold medals of USA was from Athletics, making it a stronghold for the nation. China got 8 gold medals from diving. 
-	The exploration of the highest and lowest ages of gold medallists from different disciplines shows that ages differ by sport: Athletes in sports that demands speed and power (Gymnastics, Sport Climbing) often reach the peak of their careers in their mid-20s, while athletes in tactical, precision sports with low physical loads such as Equestrian, Golf, Sailing even peaks around their 40s-50s. 

Detailed analysis is included in the Jupiter Notebook.
